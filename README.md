Indonesia has made remarkable progress in financial inclusion but the gaps tell a more important story. In this portfolio project, I analyzed World Bank Findex and OJK data to track Indonesia's financial inclusion and literacy trends from 2011 to 2024, visualized through Python and Tableau. 

While overall inclusion reached 75% and literacy hit 65%, the data reveals persistent divides. The poorest 40% lag 12 points behind the richest 60% in bank account ownership, mobile financial adoption sits at just 22%, and Indonesia ranks 4th in ASEAN behind Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam. For policymakers, these gaps signal where intervention is needed most.

 For fintechs and financial institutions, they represent one of Southeast Asia's largest untapped opportunities.
